About This Project

Project Overview
Predict and Classify Knee Osteoarthritis from X-Ray Imagery

This system uses a pre-trained MobileNetV2 deep learning model (transfer learning) to classify knee X-ray images into 5 severity grades based on the Kellgren-Lawrence (KL) grading scale — the international standard for osteoarthritis assessment.

Knee osteoarthritis (OA) is a degenerative joint disease affecting millions worldwide. Early and accurate grading from X-ray images enables timely intervention, potentially slowing disease progression and improving patient outcomes.

Kellgren-Lawrence (KL) Grading Scale
Grade 0 — Normal

No radiographic features of OA. Normal joint space, smooth bone contours, no osteophytes.

Grade 1 — Doubtful

Questionable presence of OA. Possible slight joint space narrowing and minute osteophyte formation.

Grade 2 — Mild

Definite osteophytes and possible joint space narrowing. Early degenerative changes visible.

Grade 3 — Moderate

Multiple moderate osteophytes, definite narrowing, some sclerosis, possible bone deformity.

Grade 4 — Severe

Large osteophytes, marked narrowing, severe sclerosis, definite bone deformity. May need surgery.

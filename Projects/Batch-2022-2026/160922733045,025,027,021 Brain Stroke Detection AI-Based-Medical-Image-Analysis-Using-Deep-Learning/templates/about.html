About This Project

Project Overview
AI-Based Medical Image Analysis Using Deep Learning

This system uses a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to detect brain strokes from CT scan images. Brain stroke is a medical emergency that occurs when blood supply to the brain is interrupted, leading to brain cell death. Early detection through CT imaging and AI-powered analysis can significantly improve patient outcomes.

The system classifies CT brain images into two categories: Normal (healthy brain) and Stroke Detected (signs of stroke present).

Types of Brain Stroke
Ischemic Stroke

~87% of all strokes

Caused by a blood clot blocking an artery in the brain. Appears as dark (hypodense) patches on CT scans, indicating tissue death from lack of blood flow.

Hemorrhagic Stroke

~13% of all strokes

Caused by a blood vessel rupturing in the brain. Appears as bright (hyperdense) regions on CT scans due to blood pooling in brain tissue.

Transient Ischemic Attack

"Mini Stroke"

Temporary blockage of blood flow. Symptoms resolve within 24 hours, but it serves as a warning sign for potential future strokes.

CNN Architecture
Feature Extraction Layers
Input: 1 x 128 x 128 (Grayscale CT image)
Conv2D Layer 1: 32 filters (3x3) + ReLU + MaxPool(2x2)
Conv2D Layer 2: 64 filters (3x3) + ReLU + MaxPool(2x2)
Conv2D Layer 3: 128 filters (3x3) + ReLU + MaxPool(2x2)
Conv2D Layer 4: 256 filters (3x3) + ReLU + MaxPool(2x2)
Classification Layers
Flatten: 256 x 8 x 8 = 16,384 features
Dense Layer 1: 256 neurons + ReLU + Dropout(0.5)
Dense Layer 2: 1 neuron + Sigmoid
Output: Probability (0.0 - 1.0)
